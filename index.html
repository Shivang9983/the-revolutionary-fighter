Lahore, 1907 — 1931

Bhagat Singh

A mind sharpened by books.
A resolve that never bent.

Scroll
I.

The Book Before the Bomb

Long before he was a martyr's name on a street sign, he was a reader — devouring Marx, Bakunin, and the histories of revolutions far from Punjab. He believed, ahead of his time, that a revolution was not the throwing of a bomb but a turning over of the social order, built first in the mind before it ever touched the street.

II.

Twenty-Three Years

Tried under colonial law, he used his own trial as a platform — speaking not to escape the noose but to make sure the courtroom itself stood accused. He walked to the gallows in March 1931, twenty-three years old, having spent more of his short life in study and protest than in fear.

Revolution does not necessarily involve sanguinary strife. It is not a cult of bomb and pistol.

— Bhagat Singh, Statement in the Assembly Bomb Case, 1929

III.
